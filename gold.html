Maely Gold

Our daughter, Maely Gold, was born with a rare leg difference called Fibular Hemimelia. Because her fibula is missing, her ankle and foot did not fully develop. On May 10th, she will have her foot amputated, and a few weeks later, she will be fitted for her first prosthesis. This journey has been nothing short of inspiring so far, and we cannot wait to share more of her story with the world.

Grandpa Gene’s Ironman

On May 14, 2016, Maely’s grandpa, Gene Pflederer, will be racing in his fifth full Ironman in The Woodlands, Texas. He’ll be running to support Maely and Shriners and has already hit his goal of $5,000! We are super grateful to everyone who donated. View the fundraising page here and feel free to keep donating!

Maely’s Shriners fund

We’re not exactly sure how it will all play out at this point, but we have good family medical insurance so we are not expecting heavy out-of-pocket expenses. We have been told that while Shriners will bill their patients, they don’t hassle you if you don’t pay. That said, we want to do everything we can to support Shriners financially and to increase visibility into how they are helping our family and other families by telling this story. Any money we receive as a result of Maely’s shirt sales and donations will go directly to Shriners.

If you have any questions, send us an email.

Thank you for loving and supporting Maely!
